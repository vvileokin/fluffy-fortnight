# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users
Ukrainian CS2 community — fans and semi-competitive players who follow professional Counter-Strike 2. They arrive on mobile first, often mid-tournament, to check live and upcoming matches, answer prediction questions, and see where they rank. Public content (tournaments, matches, stats, leaderboards) is readable without an account; answering questions, saving bracket simulations, entering giveaways, and managing a profile require sign-in.

## Product Purpose
A public prediction and community platform where the core value is interactive questions and forecasts tied to real matches and tournament stages. Statistics, schedules, and brackets give context for decisions; points and leaderboards create competitive motivation. Success is measured by prediction participation and repeat engagement across a tournament, not by raw traffic.

## Positioning
CS2 UA — the largest CS2 community in Ukraine («найбільша спільнота з CS2 в Україні»). Every screen leads with a decision the user can make (a prediction, a bracket pick) rather than passive content.

## Brand Personality
Dark, modern, data-dense esports. Confident and competitive without shouting. The interface reads like a broadcast analytics desk: precise numbers, clear match state, disciplined use of the brand yellow for the one thing that matters on a screen. Ukrainian-first voice. No emoji; a single structural icon set.

## Anti-references
Not casino / betting-skin aesthetics (no coin-drop glitz, no neon gambling gradients). Not a generic SaaS-cream dashboard. Not a cluttered scoreboard wall where everything competes for attention. Yellow is a scalpel, not a highlighter — never a large fill behind text.

## Design Principles
- **Decision-first.** Predictions and picks are the hero on every relevant screen; scores, veto, rosters, and stats are context that visually yields to them.
- **Broadcast clarity.** Match state (live / upcoming / final), deadlines, and points are legible at a glance, in tabular figures, from across a room.
- **Yellow is a signal.** The brand accent marks active navigation, the primary action, and points — nothing decorative.
- **Earned familiarity.** Standard app affordances (nav, tabs, filters, tables) behave exactly as expected; craft lives in tuning, density, and motion, not invented controls.
- **Mobile is the home field.** Bottom nav, thumb-reachable actions, and no horizontal scroll are non-negotiable; desktop expands the same system into a fixed sidebar.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA contrast for all text and UI. Full keyboard navigation with a visible focus ring, minimum 44×44px touch targets, consistent heading order, no horizontal scroll at 375/768/1024/1440. All motion (150–300ms) respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Ukrainian and English localization, Ukrainian default.
